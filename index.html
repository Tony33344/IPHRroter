Course Structure

This course examines the international protection of human rights through three interconnected pillars:

Norms

Treaties, declarations, and customary law that define human rights

Institutions

Bodies that monitor, interpret, and enforce human rights

Mechanisms

Procedures for reporting, complaints, and accountability

Essential Concepts

Margin of Appreciation

Latitude given to states in implementing Convention rights, recognizing local conditions and democratic choices. Narrower where European consensus exists.

ECtHR doctrine

Living Instrument

The Convention must be interpreted in light of present-day conditions, not 1950 standards. Allows dynamic, evolutive interpretation.

Tyrer v. UK (1978)

Positive Obligations

States must not only refrain from violations but actively take measures to protect rights. Includes duty to investigate, prevent, and provide remedies.

Osman v. UK (1998)

Non-Refoulement

Absolute prohibition on returning anyone to a place where they face torture or serious harm. Cannot be balanced against national security.

Soering v. UK (1989)

Three-Part Test

Limitations on qualified rights must be: (1) prescribed by law, (2) pursue legitimate aim, (3) necessary in democratic society (proportionate).

Articles 8-11 ECHR

Progressive Realization

States must take steps to maximum of available resources to progressively achieve full realization of ESC rights. But minimum core obligations are immediate.

Article 2 ICESCR

UPR Basics

  • Reviews ALL 193 UN members every 4.5 years
  • Peer review by states (not experts)
  • Three documents: national report, UN compilation, stakeholder summary
  • Troika facilitates review

Key Concepts

  • Accepted vs Noted recommendations
  • Mid-term reporting
  • NGO shadow reports
  • Implementation tracking

Exam Preparation Tips

Know the Structure

Understand the relationship between UN system (global) and regional systems (European, Inter-American, African). Know which bodies do what.

Connect Norms & Mechanisms

For each right, know: which treaty protects it, which body monitors it, what procedures exist for complaints.

Learn Key Cases

Know landmark ECtHR cases and what principles they established. Be able to apply them to new situations.

Practice Analysis

Use the three-part test for limitations. Consider margin of appreciation. Apply proportionality analysis.
